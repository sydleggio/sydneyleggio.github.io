Astrometric GW Detection
Correlated Proper Motion Anomalies from the SGWB
Gravitational waves leave a quadrupolar pattern in the proper motions of distant sources. I am developing statistical estimators to extract this signal from Gaia and future Roman/Theia catalogues.

SGWB Characterization
Spectral Shape & Anisotropy of the GW Background
Constraining the spectral index and angular power spectrum of the SGWB using angular correlations in astrometric residuals, complementary to pulsar timing array measurements.
Read more →
SMBHB Population
Supermassive Binary Black Hole Demography
Linking the SGWB amplitude and spectral shape to the underlying population of inspiralling supermassive binaries across cosmic time — their mass function, merger history, and galactic environment.

About

The Researcher

[Photo]

I am a PhD candidate in astrophysics at University of Southern California, advised by Prof. [Advisor Name]. My research sits at the intersection of precision astrometry and gravitational wave cosmology, using the positions of distant stars and quasars as a galactic-scale detector for the stochastic gravitational wave background.

The nanohertz SGWB — recently indicated by pulsar timing arrays — encodes a wealth of information about the supermassive binary black hole population and early-universe physics. I am working to extract complementary constraints from upcoming astrometric surveys including Gaia, Roman, and Theia.

Before my PhD I completed my BSc in Physics at [Undergraduate Institution], where I became captivated by the idea that the universe's entire gravitational wave history might be hiding inside a catalogue of stellar positions.
