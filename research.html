Current Projects

Developing novel astrometric probes of the stochastic gravitational wave background sourced by the cosmic population of supermassive binary black holes.

Astrometric GW Detection
Correlated Proper Motion Anomalies from the SGWB
Gravitational waves leave a quadrupolar pattern in the proper motions of distant sources. I am developing statistical estimators to extract this signal from Gaia and future Roman/Theia catalogues. This involves computing the Hellings–Downs-like correlation function for astrometric observables and building matched-filter pipelines robust to systematic astrometric errors.
SGWB Characterization
Spectral Shape & Anisotropy of the GW Background
Constraining the spectral index and angular power spectrum of the SGWB using angular correlations in astrometric residuals, complementary to pulsar timing array measurements. The astrometric signal probes a different frequency band and sky-weighting than PTAs, enabling joint constraints on SGWB anisotropy and spectral shape.
SMBHB Population
Supermassive Binary Black Hole Demography
Linking the SGWB amplitude and spectral shape to the underlying population of inspiralling supermassive binaries across cosmic time — their mass function, merger history, and galactic environment. Bayesian hierarchical models connect GW observations to galaxy evolution and black hole co-evolution.
