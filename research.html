Current Projects

CosmoLab
Detecting SGWB Using Astrometry
Gravitational waves leave a quadrupolar imprint on the proper motions of stars across the sky. I am developing the angular power spectrum formalism to extract this signal, building toy models to derive optimal estimators for the astrometric setting, and characterizing what information real surveys like the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope can realistically recover once survey geometry and noise are taken into account.
UltraViolet EXplorer
UVEX Gravitational Wave Follow-Up
Optimized electromagnetic follow-up strategies for gravitational wave-detected binary neutron star mergers by simulating observing scenarios tailored to UVEX's capabilities — targeting kilonova light curves within critical detection windows. Statistical modeling of survey efficiency and cadence connected telescope design choices to maximized follow-up prospects for transient astrophysical events, with simulations extended to the upcoming ULTRASAT mission.

SmallSat Research Laboratory
Small Satellite Project Manager
Led the development of two NSF- and AFRL-funded space missions — IMPRESS and EXACT — within the Small Satellite Research Laboratory, overseeing hardware-software integration through Gantt chart maintenance, critical path mapping, and optimized testing and documentation workflows. Drove EXACT through a significant design transition from a free-flying CubeSat to a hosted payload architecture, while coordinating cross-functional teams and ensuring compliance with UNP and NSF mission deadlines.
